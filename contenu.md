General description of the Master
 The Master in Digital Transition at Université Paris 8 trains professionals to design and manage responsible digital transitions by integrating technological, social, and organizational perspectives. It prepares students to co‑create inclusive and sustainable digital solutions with diverse stakeholders in public, private, and civic contexts.
Overview of courses and specialisations
 The programme combines theoretical and applied courses in digital technologies, transition management, user research, and participatory design. Students choose specialised pathways according to their interests, focusing on areas such as digital transformation in organisations, public services, cultural institutions, or territorial projects.
Pedagogical methods (projects, internships, partnerships)
 Teaching is project‑based and collaborative, with students working on real‑world digital transition projects carried out in partnership with local authorities, public institutions, NGOs, and private organisations. The curriculum includes supervised internships, research or practice‑based projects, and regular collaboration with external partners, ensuring close links between academic learning and professional practice.
Admissions & Candidatures
Admission criteria
 Admission to the Master in Digital Transition is granted to candidates holding a Bachelor’s degree (or equivalent, 180 ECTS) in fields related to communication, digital humanities, information and communication sciences, sociology, geography, administration, or digital technologies. Applications are assessed on the basis of academic background, motivation, and relevance of the candidate’s project to the programme’s focus on responsible and participatory digital transitions.
Application process
Candidates must submit an online application via the university’s admission platform, including their CV, academic transcripts, a motivation letter, and, where applicable, proof of language proficiency. Shortlisted applicants may be invited to an interview or to present their project before the admissions committee.
Key dates
 Applications typically open in the spring for entry in the following academic year, with a first deadline for early applicants and a final deadline for standard candidates. Exact dates are published annually and may vary depending on the candidate’s status (EU/non‑EU, continuing education, apprenticeship, distance learning).

Careers accessible after the Master
Graduates of the Master in Digital Transition are prepared for roles at the intersection of digital technologies, social transformation, and project management. Typical career paths include digital transition officer, project manager in digital transformation, user experience and service designer, digital strategy consultant, coordinator of digital innovation projects in public services, NGOs, cultural institutions, and private companies, as well as roles in digital mediation and participatory design.
Partnerships with companies and organisations (AJT DES ORGANISATIONS)
The programme develops close partnerships with public institutions, local authorities, cultural organisations, NGOs, and private companies involved in digital innovation and transition projects. These partnerships support teaching through case studies, guest lectures, live projects, internships, and joint research initiatives, enabling students to engage with real‑world challenges and to build professional networks before graduation.

Associations, events and networking
 Students benefit from an active ecosystem of student associations, research groups, and professional networks related to digital technologies, transition and social innovation. Regular events such as workshops, conferences, hackathons, and project days bring together students, researchers, practitioners, and industry partners, fostering collaboration, knowledge exchange, and career opportunities in the field of responsible digital transitions.

WHAT MAKES THIS MASTER DIFFERENT ?
Transdisciplinary approach to digital transition
 The Master in Digital Transition brings together humanities, social sciences, information and communication studies, law, economics, computer science, and ergonomics, enabling students to analyse and act on digital change from multiple perspectives.
Strong focus on responsible and participatory transitions
 The programme emphasises co‑design, user‑centred methods, and ethical considerations in digital transformation projects, preparing graduates to support responsible, inclusive, and socially grounded transitions in organisations and territories.
Project‑based pedagogy rooted in real cases
 Teaching is organised around concrete projects with public institutions, local authorities, NGOs, and private companies, often in partnership with actors such as Cap Digital, so that students gain practical experience before entering the labour market.
Diverse and flexible learning pathways
 The Master offers full‑time, work‑based learning (apprenticeship), continuing education, and distance‑learning options, making it accessible to students from different academic backgrounds and professional situations.
Close ties with research and innovation ecosystems
 The programme is embedded within the Master in Digital Humanities at Université Paris 8 and benefits from links with research laboratories (e.g. Paragraphe) and initiatives such as the EUR ArTeC, fostering a strong connection between academic research and innovation practices.
International and critical digital watch
 The programme integrates international digital watch and critical analysis of digital trends, helping students move beyond media‑driven hype and build a solid, interdisciplinary knowledge base for informed decision‑making.
