What Makes This Master Different?

Shaping responsible digital futures through transdisciplinary practice.

01

Transdisciplinary approach

The Master brings together humanities, social sciences, information and communication studies, law, economics, computer science, and ergonomics, enabling students to analyse and act on digital change from multiple perspectives.

02

Responsible & participatory transitions

The programme emphasises co-design, user-centred methods, and ethical considerations in digital transformation projects, preparing graduates to support responsible, inclusive, and socially grounded transitions.

03

Project-based pedagogy

Teaching is organised around concrete projects with public institutions, local authorities, NGOs, and private companies, so that students gain practical experience before entering the labour market.

04

Diverse & flexible pathways

The Master offers full-time, work-based learning (apprenticeship), and continuing education making it accessible to students from different backgrounds and professional situations.

05

Research & innovation ecosystems

Embedded within the Master in Digital Humanities at Paris 8 with links to research laboratories (e.g. Paragraphe) and initiatives such as the EUR ArTeC, fostering a strong connection between research and practice.

06

International & critical digital watch

The programme integrates international digital watch and critical analysis of digital trends, helping students move beyond media-driven hype and build a solid, interdisciplinary knowledge base.

Q&A

What kind of background?

We value interdisciplinarity. Whether you come from Humanities, Computer Science, Law, or Psychology, we are looking for curious minds interested in the societal impact of digital transitions.

How many students?

To ensure high-quality support and project-based learning, the capacity is limited to 30 students for M1 and 30 for M2.

Does this master welcome international student?

Yes! The Master Transitions Numériques aims to strengthen its European and international visibility. We encourage applications from students across Europe and beyond.

Why choose this master?

Why choose this master?

  • A unique Blend : a rare mix of coding, digital law and social sciences.
  • You work on concrete projects with local partners like Ville des Lilas, MNFRD, and international companies such as OpenAI
  • We prioritize apprenticeships (alternance) and direct coaching from tech professionals.
  • Join a Master that values personal reflection and encourages you to "dream of having dreams".

What makes this master different?

Unlike purely theoretical courses, we focus on Project-Based Learning. You will work in interdisciplinary teams on real-life cases. We also close the gap between Social Sciences and technical skills like coding.

Who are the teachers?

The team is a mix of high-level academic researchers (Laboratoire Paragraphe) and professional experts from the AI and Blockchain ecosystems.

What skills will I learn?

The curriculum is adaptive: you can start from scratch or deepen your knowledge in Python, Front-end/Back-end development, No-code tools, Digital focused classes such as Psychology, communication, law... See more about the class →

Can I do apprenticeships?

Yes, it is highly recommended for both M1 and M2. We partner with CFA Numia to help you secure a position.

What companies do students work for?

Our students find placements in various sectors:

  • Tech & Innovation: AI and software companies.
  • Public Sector: Ministries and local authorities.
  • Consulting: Digital strategy and compliance agencies.
  • Partnerships: We have worked with names like Ville des Lilas, MNFRD, certiphy.io, and OpenAI.

Admissions & Applications

01

Admission Criteria

Admission is granted to candidates holding a Bachelor's degree (or equivalent, 180 ECTS) in fields related to communication, digital humanities, information and communication sciences, sociology, geography, administration, or digital technologies. Applications are assessed on academic background, motivation, and relevance of the candidate's project.

02

Application Process

Submit an application via Monmaster or Campus France, with your curriculum, academic transcripts, a motivation letter, and a proof of language proficiency.

03

Key Dates

Applications typically open in the spring for entry in the following academic year, with an early deadline and a standard deadline. Exact dates are published annually and may vary depending on candidate status (EU/non-EU, continuing education, apprenticeship, distance learning).
